## Summary  
We consider this governance action to be constitutional.

## Rationale Statement  
This proposal is a Treasury Withdrawal Governance Action to withdraw ₳1,768,167 from the Cardano Treasury to fund EMURGO’s “Cardano at TOKEN2049 Singapore 2026: Top-Up ‘Title’ Sponsorship Upgrade.” Regarding Article 2, Section 6, Paragraphs 1 and 2 of the Cardano Constitution, this proposal adopts an immutable off-chain reference using IPFS. Furthermore, the proposal text includes the title, summary, rationale, event objectives, KPIs, event program, budget, milestones, management structure, audit and reporting framework, risks, and a Constitutional Self-Assessment; we therefore determine that it presents information sufficient for evaluation as a Governance Action. Regarding Article 2, Section 7, Paragraphs 1 and 2, this proposal aims to upgrade to “Title” sponsorship at TOKEN2049 Singapore 2026. It outlines plans to enhance the Cardano ecosystem’s visibility, facilitate networking, foster partnerships, and drive ecosystem growth through mainstage keynotes, media introductions, branding expansion, and a larger booth footprint. Additionally, the proposal details the event dates (October 7–8, 2026), budget breakdown, refund conditions, and the conditions for returning unused funds and surplus ADA in the event of an ADA price increase. Furthermore, the proposal specifies that it is a top-up proposal based on the “Baseline ‘Platinum’ Sponsorship Proposal” and includes a condition that 100% of the funds will be returned to the Treasury if the baseline proposal is not approved. Furthermore, the proposal details the history of past Treasury funding received. Regarding Article 2, Section 7, Paragraph 3, the requested amount for this proposal is ₳1,768,167, which falls within the 350M ADA Net Change Limit applicable to Epochs 613–713 at the time of submission. The proposal also confirms that even when combined with the baseline proposal, the total amount is ₳5,071,917, which remains within the remaining NCL limit. Furthermore, the requested amount for this proposal is stated in ADA, and the proposal also includes a USD equivalent based on an estimated ADA price at the time of writing. Article 2, Section 7, Paragraphs 4 and 5 describe treasury administration with Intersect as the administrator; the Treasury Reserve Smart Contract (TRSC) and Project-Specific Smart Contracts (PSSC); the Oversight Committee; third-party guarantees; audits; reporting obligations; and the visualization of KPIs, fund transfers, and smart contract management status via a public dashboard. It also describes the use of treasury management smart contracts by Sundae Labs. Furthermore, Article 2, Section 7, Paragraph 6 describes fund management using TRSC and PSSC, non-delegation to SPOs, and delegation to the auto-abstain DRep. We determine that this structure is intended to align with the requirements of “auditable by the Cardano Community,” “separate accounts,” “not delegated to a SPO,” and “delegated to a predefined abstain voting option.” Therefore, as no clear conflict with the Cardano Constitution has been identified, we determine that this proposal is constitutional.

## Precedent Discussion  
None

## Counterargument Discussion  
It is difficult to say that a structure in which funds for multiple projects are consolidated into the TRSC using a single receiving address is necessarily consistent with the requirement for “separate accounts” set forth in Article 2, Section 7, Paragraph 6 of the Constitution. In light of this, some of our members abstained or considered the structure to be unconstitutional, citing the difficulty of determining its constitutionality.

## Conclusion  
For the reasons stated above, we determine that it is constitutional.
